My Favorite Recipe.

Chilla is an unleavened Indian pancake made with lentil or whole grain flour. The sweet version has jaggery and cardamoms in it while the savory version has some spices, herbs, tomatoes and onions

why it's my favorite?.

Nutrient-Rich:
Chillas can be packed with essential nutrients,including protein, vitamins (like C),and minerals (like iron and potassium),according to Instagram. For example,moong dal chilla is a good source of these nutrients, says Earthy Origins. 
Heart Health:
Fiber and potassium, found in some chillas like moong dal chilla, can help lower cholesterol levels and regulate blood pressure, notes Instagram.

How to make chilla

  1. If you are feeling experimental, then you can go for a cheese, paneer or chopped veggies filling for your chilla and add your own twist to it.
  2. Make sure that you fry the chilla on medium heat to ensure that it cooks well from the inside too.
  3. Add rawa to your besan chilla batter if you want to make them crisp and crunchy.
  4. For people who are allergic to gluten, make sure that you use pure gram flour as it is gluten-free.
  5. If you want to make your chilla healthier, you can add spinach or grated carrot, or beetroot into the batter.
  6. You can add ajwain seeds to your chilla batter if you want to make your chilla healthier, as ajwain is known to aid digestion and give a pleasant aroma to your chilla. You can also add grated ginger to your chilla to further increase its nutritional value.
